# Color Theory for Interior Design

## Color wheel relationships
- **Complementary**: Opposite on the wheel (e.g. blue and orange) — high contrast, use in small doses.
- **Analogous**: Adjacent colors (e.g. blue, blue-green, green) — harmonious, easy to live with.
- **Triadic**: Three colors evenly spaced — vibrant; balance one dominant, two accent.

## Warm vs cool palettes
- **Warm**: Reds, oranges, yellows — cozy, energizing; good for living rooms and dining.
- **Cool**: Blues, greens, purples — calm, spacious; good for bedrooms and bathrooms.

## The 60-30-10 rule
Use 60% dominant color (walls, large furniture), 30% secondary (upholstery, curtains), 10% accent (pillows, art, accessories). Keeps the room balanced and intentional.
